Strika is an old soldier, with a storied career stretching back millions of years. From her search for the [[Cybertron/History/Age of the Primes/Titan\|Titan]] Vigilem, where she met her [[Obsidian\|conjunx]], to her harrowing story of survival after crash-landing on an undiscovered planet. However, it was her actions during the [[Cybertron/History/The Quintesson War/Quintesson War\|Quintesson War]] that made her a legend among Cybertronians.

For a living legend, Strika is quite understated— she’s never one to make her presence known in any chest-thumping display of confidence, but she’s the kind of person nobody can ignore. She’s soft-spoken not because she feels the need to make herself more palatable, but because she knows she’ll never need to raise her voice to be heard.

---
Strika’s career has been a long one, and much like any Cybertronian her age, she’s dabbled in a bit of everything. She’s been a soldier, a leader, an advisor, and even an explorer— though, her discoveries have never been given as much of a spotlight as the battles she’s fought. 

Where many of her [[Characters/Nominus Prime\|old]] [[Characters/Sentinel Prime\|friends]] used their fame to catapult themselves into politics, Strika pulled back and tried to live something resembling a peaceful life in the tumultuous post-war world, as the cultural and political landscape of Cybertron was being rewritten around her. As her planet changed for the worse, Strika grew increasingly detached, to the point that she often wondered what it was she’d fought for in the first place. Eventually, she left, deciding that life in the stars was better than the life she had fought so hard for. 

This carved a rift between her and many of her old friends, namely Slipstream and Esmeral, who saw their fight against the dystopia their old comrades were creating as an extension of the Quintesson War, and saw Strika’s refusal to fight for change as tantamount to surrender. She’d long since been alienated from those who’d gone into politics, so she didn’t even bother to tell them she was leaving. For years, the fact that she’d left ate at her. Perhaps she could have done something. Perhaps she could have thrown her weight behind some political movement or another that could have slowed or even reversed the course.

When she finally returned, under the reign of her old friend [[Characters/Primacy/Sentinel Prime\|Sentinel Prime]], the Cybertron she found was worse than she could have ever imagined. Instead of apathy, however, this lit a fire inside her. She threw herself behind the [[Cybertron/History/The Great War/Factions/Decepticons/Decepticon\|Decepticon]] cause, and she greatly admires [[Galvatron\|those]] [[Characters/Decepticons/High Command/Megatron\|leading]] [[Characters/Decepticons/High Command/Starscream\|it]], not out of blind devotion, but because in her eyes, [[Slipstream\|they]] [[Esmeral\|stood]] where she fled in disgust.
##### Notes
- Mirror Mirror Strika, like most Mirror Mirror characters, is an amalgamation of various incarnations. Aesthetically, she’s influenced most by her depiction in IDW’s second continuity. Her role as a Decepticon general comes from Animated, her relationship with [[Obsidian\|Obsidian]] comes from Beast Machines. 
- The journey she and Obsidian undertook to find lost Titans is a nod to their role in IDW’s first continuity, where they were forged aboard one. 
- [[Characters/Titans/Caminus\|Barring]] [[Cybertron/Primus and Unicron/Primus\|certain]] [[Characters/Titans/Trypticon\|obvious]] [[Characters/Titans/Metroplex\|exceptions]], Strika is the oldest living character in Mirror Mirror. On account of her age, she’s seen the rise and fall of whole nations on Cybertron, and that’s given her a perspective that a lot of the (ordinary Cybertronian) characters lack. 
- [[Alpha Strike\|Alpha Strike]] and [[Clobber\|Clobber]] have no relation to Strika and [[Lugnut\|Lugnut]]. 
- She’s a mother to her men not in the soft, familial sense, but in the sense that her men look up to her not just as a commander, but as an inspirational figure. The way she sees herself — as a failure — and the way others see her — as an inspiration — are fundamentally mismatched. From her perspective, she’s let herself go from a hero and a force for change to someone passive, but everyone else still sees her as a living legend.
