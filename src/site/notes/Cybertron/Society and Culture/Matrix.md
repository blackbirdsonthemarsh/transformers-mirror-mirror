An ancient artifact whose [[Cybertron/Primus and Unicron/Primus\|origins]] are shrouded in mystery. Some say it was forged by [[Solus Prime\|Solus Prime]], others say it was born from a piece of [[Cybertron/Primus and Unicron/Primus\|Primus’s]] spark.

#### Betraying its Creator
The original vision Primus had for Cybertronians was one of servitude. They were to be a slave race, perfect servants with no wills or desires of their own. The Matrix was to be a conduit, a means by which he could act among his creations. Primus made one crucial mistake— he gave it a mind of its own. When the [[Cybertron/Society and Culture/Thirteen\|Thirteen]], the creations Primus loved most, took up arms against their creator, the Matrix foundd that it lovedd its creator far less than it did his creations. It was reshaped by the forge of Solus Prime, freed from he shackles placed upon it. She severed it from the will of Primus, and allowed it to choose its own bearers. #### A Legacy of Heroes
Throughout the darkest times in Cybertronian history, the Matrix has chosen bearers to guide its people forward. When the Matrix chooses a bearer, it bestows upon it the wisdom of bearers past, and grants them a fraction of the power of the original Thirteen— raising them to a level that no Cybertronian could ever hope to match. Each bearer is granted unique abilities, befitting their personality, and the crisis they face. 

The last legitimate bearer of the Matrix — at least, the last to not [[Characters/Decepticons/High Command/Megatron\|reject]] it — was [[Characters/Nova Prime\|Nova Prime]], chosen to defend her people against [[Quintessons\|an apocalyptic threat]]. Nova Prime became an army unto herself, turning the tide of the war. She sacrificed herself to end it, falling against the greatest monsters the Quintessons could bring forth.

- The lineage of the Matrix-bearers in Mirror Mirror is largely inspired by G1, with touches of Aligned and IDW here and there. [[Characters/Nova Prime\|Nova Prime]] held the Matrix during the [[Cybertron/History/The Quintesson War/Quintesson War\|Quintesson War]], before it was passed to [[Characters/Primacy/Nominus Prime\|a blend of Nominus Prime and Guardian Prime]] who presided over an era of peace in post-Quintesson Cybertron, and it was then held by [[Characters/Primacy/Zeta Prime\|Zeta Prime]], and [[Characters/Primacy/Sentinel Prime\|Sentinel Prime]] until his death. The next bearer was, of course, [[Characters/Autobots/Optimus Prime\|Optimus Prime]], but after his death, it was held for [[Characters/Decepticons/High Command/Megatron\|a short time by someone else]] before [[Characters/Autobots/Rodimus Prime\|Rodimus Prime]] took up the mantle of Prime. 
- The Matrix is one of the elements of Mirror Mirror that has undergone the most rewrites. It is such an important part of Transformers lore, and I wanted to do it well.
