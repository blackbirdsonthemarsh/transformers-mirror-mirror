Short for “made-to-order.” A term referring to a Cybertronian who is [[Cybertron/Cybertronian Biology/Cold Construct\|constructed cold]] for the sole purpose of being a - typically disposable - soldier. MTOs are mass-produced as cheaply as possible. The archetypal MTO is an infantryman with a life expectancy of three minutes, but MTOs have been built to fill a wide variety of roles, from medical staff to transportation. Much like pre-war cold constructs, MTOs are not officially named, instead being given a designation indicating their function.

#### The Primacy
In the wake of the [[Cybertron/History/Decepticon Uprising\|Decepticon Uprising]] and during the wars of independence that followed, the Primacy found itself with an increasing lack of manpower, amplified by the fact that it had to stretch its already meager forces across the whole of Cybertron. The sophisticated (or some said, bloated) military [[Characters/Nominus Prime\|three]] [[Characters/Primacy/Zeta Prime\|generations]] [[Characters/Sentinel Prime\|of]] leaders had built up had deserted it, and millions of years of repression left the populace apathetic towards its existence at best. Thus, Sentinel Prime ordered the cities still loyal to — or controlled by — the Primacy to use whatever sparks and pre-fab frames they had in storage, in a desperate attempt to stave off the inevitable. These soldiers — referred to derisively as “made-to-order” by their commanders — were deployed with little to no training, and little to no understanding of why they were even fighting in the first place. Morale was poor, and many of these soldiers deserted or [[Characters/Ambulon\|defected]] as soon as they were able. The program was, by all accounts, a miserable failure that some historians argue did not even meaningfully halt the fall of the Primacy. 
#### The Great War
The Autobots made heavy use of MTOs throughout the war, with production peaking in the middle of the war, slowly tapering off due to growing resource shortages. Early-war MTOs were much larger than their late-war counterparts, who, in addition to being smaller, tended to be more error-ridden, lacking basic features, such as faces, to save on costs. 

Initially, MTOs were meant to be a supplement, to make up the difference in manpower between the Autobots and Decepticons, but as civilian recruitment dried up, the army began to rely on them more and more.
