Technology that allows [[Cybertronians\|Cybertronians]] to shunt the mass of an object into [[Subspace\|subspace]], either to make it lighter, or change its size.

Mass-shifting is not an innate characteristic of Cybertronians (save [[Cybertron/Cybertronian Biology/Outlier\|outliers]], who may possess such an ability). Ergo, a Cybertronian’s size and weight in robot mode is determined by their vehicle mode. A tank will be much shorter than a fighter jet, but much heavier. Some time before the [[Cybertron/History/The Quintesson War/Quintesson War\|Quintesson War]], a simple version was invented that allowed a weapon to be paired with its wielder’s spark, allowing them to shunt its mass into subspace. This allowed a one to wield a monumentally heavy weapon as if it was a fairly light one, but meant that weapon could only be feasibly wielded by its intended user, and was effectively useless after their death. The technology never took off, being relegated to expensive custom weapons. 

In the distant future, mass-shifting is a common upgrade installed in Cybertronians, allowing anyone to change size at will. 
##### Notes
- This means that, barring the distant, distant future that [[Cyclonus\|Cyclonus]] comes from, everyone’s size is dependent on their alternate mode. This has been very fun to work with from a writing and worldbuilding perspective, but it has also meant that I’ve had to limit the amount of alternate modes characters can have. Microscopes, tape decks, data slugs, etc. are out the window, as the characters would be *too* small to function in the story. Motorcycles are the lower limit.
