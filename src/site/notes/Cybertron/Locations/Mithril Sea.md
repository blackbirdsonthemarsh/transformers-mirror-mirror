The largest and deepest sea on Cybertron. Nestled along its shore is the city of [[Cybertron/Polities/Kalis\|Kalis]] and the mountains of the [[Cybertron/Locations/Tagan Heights\|Tagan Heights]]. [[Cybertron/Polities/Vos\|Vos]] floats above its shores, its islands stretching out over the water.

The Mithril Sea is is home to some pf the most diverse ecosystems on Cybertron. Most famously, it is home to aquatic megafauna, some being thousands of feet in length. These megafauna — alongside all life in the Mithril Sea — emerge from hot spots on the surface, and slowly migrate upwards as they mature. The carnivorous species are colloquially referred to as “sea beasts.” 

Above the surface of the sea is a thriving ecosystem of flying autotrophs, deriving sustenance from in-built solar panels and turbines. They spend their entire lives in the air, coasting along the sea breeze. They’re gentle creatures, and so it’s been a pastime of many a young flier to try and land on their backs. 

The sea is a major trade artery in the northern hemisphere, and many of the most prosperous cities on its shores gained their fortune from maritime trade. However, the sea is treacherous, in large part due to its wildlife. Younger sea beasts will mistake Cybertronians for their preferred prey, and desperate ones will be driven to hunt them. Trading vessels are traditionally accompanied by armed escorts, and few brave the waters alone. 

The seaside cities are guarded by massive sea walls, lined with state-of-the-art weaponry and sophisticated detection systems. For some seaside states like [[Cybertron/Polities/Kalis\|Kalis]], sea beasts are an integral part of their culture. Among the more traditional sort, hunting them is seen as a coming-of-age ritual, marking one’s entrance into society. 

Kalis is famous for its culture of hunting, though it has begun to fall out of fashion since the [[Cybertron/History/The Quintesson War/Quintesson War\|Quintesson War]]. Many older or more traditional Kalisians maintain a collection of painted skulls, even incorporating them into the architecture of their homes. Many buildings in Kalis are built from the bodies of sea beasts, including most of the city’s administrative buildings. Provided they’re large enough, the skulls will be used for entryways. 

##### Notes
---
- Typically, the most relevant sea in Mirror Mirror is the Sea of Rust. However, outside of Beast Wars: Uprising (and perhaps a few other continuities here and there) it is rarely presented as an actual body of water, instead ranging from an apocalyptic hellscape to an ever-shifting expanse. Due to Mirror Mirror having several prominent boats (hello [[Characters/Decepticons/High Command/Thunderblast\|Thunderblast]]) I decided that its Cybertron would need actual bodies of water. I wanted to keep the Sea of Rust as something more unsettling, so I decided to go with the [Mithril Sea.](https://tfwiki.net/wiki/Mithril_Sea) Outside of its brief appearance in Beast Wars: Uprising, the vast majority of its appearances have been one-off mentions either in dialogue or toy bios, which gave me free reign to do whatever I wanted with it.
