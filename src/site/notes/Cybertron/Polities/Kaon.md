#### Society and Culture
Due to their shared history and geographic proximity, there is a great deal of cultural overlap between Kaon and Tarn. 

Their shared religion venerates [[Solus Prime\|Solus]] and [[Characters/The Thirteen/Megatronus Prime\|Megatronus Prime]] as the greatest of the Thirteen, with a particular emphasis on Solus. It does not worship them as gods, however, instead seeing them as inspirational figures that one should always strive to be like. Prayer is seen as a time for self-reflection, where one looks to the lives an deeds of the Primes, and reflects upon their own, asking themself what they have aspired to, where they have fallen short, and how they can improve themself. While there are public sites at which to pray (which also double as charities, shelters, and free kitchens where all are welcome) in Tarn and Kaon, most prayers happen inside the home, as it is meant to be an introspective process, all about self-reflection. All that’s actually required to pray is a light, ideally a flame, but anything — even moonlight — is acceptable in a pinch.

Their faith stresses that one must always leave the world a better place than they found it, and even if one cannot shoulder such a burden alone, it’s still their responsibility to try. Solus was a builder before she was a warrior, and that aspect of her is emphasized throughout their traditional philosophy. While one must take up arms in the face of injustice, that is not the only responsibility one has, nor is it their greatest one. Their most important duty is to mend the scars of the injured people. Solus did not only create fine artworks and magnificent weapons, she built the cities of Tarn and Kaon, the mountains around them, to protect the people who she held dear. 

Unlike elsewhere on Cybertron, it is traitionally held that all the Thirteen are dead. They shall not return, and in their passing, their duty has been passed to the people of Cybertron. It is the duty of the living to live up to the legacy of those who came before, and to make the world better for those who come after.
