As the ages went on, and the [[Cybertronians\|Cybertronian]] race advanced to the point that they no longer needed their Primes, the [[Cybertron/History/Age of the Primes/Thirteen\|Thirteen]] and the [[Cybertron/History/Age of the Primes/Titan\|Titans]] that had sworn fealty to them scattered to the winds, seeking adventure in far-off lands— or even other planets. [[Tempo\|Tempo]], the Titan who’d sworn an oath to [[Vector Prime\|Vector Prime]], chose to remain, even as Vector Prime departed for parts unknown. With his ability to slip in and out of time and space as easily as an organic drew breath, Tempo knew his friend would always return to him. As Tempo journeyed across Cybertron, he found friends, who chose to live upon and within him as he went. 

Eventually, Tempo carried a city on his back. 

One day, he chose to rest. What would be generations for a Cybertronian are mere moments for a Titan, and so he slumbered long enough that he became a legend, a simple regional folk tale.  

At the end of the [[Cybertron/History/The Great War/Great War\|Great War]], he heard the call of of his old friend, [[Characters/Titans/Trypticon\|Trypticon]], and woke up. 
##### Notes
- In Ask Vector Prime, we learn that Vector Prime [is quite fond of](https://tfwiki.net/wiki/Vector_Prime#As_a_multiversal_singularity) [Apophenia](https://tfwiki.net/wiki/Apophenia). Thus, it is his city, and the one that is built atop his Titan. 
- Tempo being a giant isopod came from a good friend, who pitched to me the idea of a “giant isopod war kaiju.” I couldn’t resist! Said friend also suggested that the city that lives on said isopod’s back venerate Vector Prime.
